Who Is Adele?

Adele is a British singer-songwriter who has sold millions of records across the globe and has received 15 Grammy Awards and an Academy Award. Adele's first two albums, 19 and 21, received critical acclaim and financial success that was unrivalled among her contemporaries. Adele returned to the charts in 2015 with the ballad "Hello," the first song off what was termed her comeback album 25, after having a mother in 2012. For her work on 25, she received five Grammys in 2017, including album, record, and song of the year.

Early Life, Education and Influences

Adele Laurie Blue Adkins was born in North London, England, on May 5, 1988. Adele was the sole child of Penny Adkins, a "artsy mom" who was just 18 when she gave birth, and Mark Evans, a Welsh father who abandoned the family when Adele was only four years old. Evans kept in touch with his daughter until she was in her adolescent years, when his alcoholism and growing isolation from her led their connection to deteriorate. Adele, on the other hand, had a strong relationship with her mother, who encouraged her young daughter to "explore rather than stay with one thing."
